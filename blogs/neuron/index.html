By Asim Iqbal

I am excited to share some recent updates of our successful partnership with scientists at Broad Institute, Harvard Medical School and several leading organizations. Our work, An enhancer-AAV toolbox to target and manipulate distinct interneuron subtypes, is published in Neuron (Cell Press) on May 21, 2025.

Tibbling AI That Understands Biology

By leveraging Tibbling AI platform to systematically analyze single-cell genomic data, we have identified enhancer elements that enable highly specific targeting of distinct neuronal subtypes using rAAV vectors. This led to the development of versatile enhancer-AAV tools for observing, labeling, and manipulating specific neurons, advancing both neuroscience research and potential therapeutic applications.


We built an advanced AI framework, extensively pre-trained on large-scale spatial genomics data and fine-tuned on enhancer-AAV candidates. Our custom architecture employs a composite loss function combining pixel-wise log-loss, mask cross-entropy, and Structural Similarity Index (SSIM) components to preserve delicate neuronal features.


Our model outperforms established methods including UNetr, TransUNet, UltraUNet, and MedSAM, as measured by inverted HD95 scores—the gold standard for biomedical imaging accuracy.


Our model significantly outperforms all competing neuronal segmentation methods, with our approach achieving the highest accuracy scores across industry-standard benchmarks.

Automated Brain Atlas Registration

Our AI framework also delivers fully automated, atlas-based registration using advanced feature-tracking alignment combined with AI-guided spatial transformations. Every brain section maps precisely to the Allen Mouse Brain Atlas, ensuring accurate anatomical annotation for each detected neuronal feature.

This GIF shows AI-powered brain atlas registration in action - demonstrating how multiple brain tissue sections are automatically aligned and mapped to standardized anatomical coordinates, with colored overlays representing different brain regions being precisely identified and segmented across the entire dataset.



Comparative testing showed our model significantly outperforms VoxelMorph, TransMorph, and SynthMorph—delivering the regional accuracy essential for enhancer validation studies.

This performance advantage translates directly to more reliable results, fewer false positives, and more robust scientific conclusions.
